# Venture OS Modules and Workflows

## Operating Loop

Venture OS runs a weekly founder-growth loop:

1. Monitor sources.
2. Capture signals.
3. Score opportunities.
4. Route to capital, revenue, partner, visibility, or collateral workflow.
5. Draft and prepare.
6. Review claims, finance, and approvals.
7. Execute human-approved outreach/submission.
8. Follow up and update pipeline.
9. Publish weekly founder brief.

## W1: Source Signal To Opportunity

Trigger:

- Web listener finds a new opportunity.
- LinkedIn/manual capture adds a signal.
- Newsletter/email creates a lead.
- Partner/advisor shares a warm intro.

Flow:

1. Create signal with URL, excerpt, date, organization, geography, source type.
2. Dedupe against existing signals/opportunities.
3. Score by strategic fit, timing, credibility, expected value, and effort.
4. Route to opportunity stage or research queue.
5. Assign owner and next action.

Acceptance:

- Signal has source URL and excerpt.
- Opportunity has type, score, owner, deadline, status, and next action.

## W2: Capital Opportunity Workflow

Applies to VC, grants, foundations, DFIs, accelerators, challenges, angels, and
catalytic capital.

Flow:

1. Confirm eligibility and strategic fit.
2. Identify funding amount, instrument, deadline, decision process.
3. Map required collateral.
4. Prepare outreach or application.
5. Review claims and finance.
6. Founder approves.
7. Submit/send.
8. Save evidence and schedule follow-up.

Deliverables:

- Investor brief.
- Application checklist.
- Pitch deck / one-pager.
- Financial model extract.
- Follow-up sequence.

## W3: Revenue And Pilot Workflow

Applies to paid pilots, buyer relationships, enterprise procurement, channels,
and anchor customers.

Flow:

1. Define buyer problem and product fit.
2. Identify economic buyer, user, influencer, procurement path.
3. Draft pilot scope and success criteria.
4. Create commercial ask.
5. Review pricing and terms.
6. Founder approves outreach/proposal.
7. Track meeting, proposal, negotiation, close.

Deliverables:

- Buyer brief.
- Pilot concept note.
- Commercial proposal.
- Pricing/options memo.
- Meeting prep and follow-up.

## W4: Partnership Workflow

Applies to DFIs, sovereigns, banks, telcos, universities, NGOs, standards bodies,
trade associations, and corporate programs.

Flow:

1. Classify partner type and strategic value.
2. Map relationship path and decision structure.
3. Define partnership thesis and mutual value.
4. Draft concept note or intro.
5. Review policy/regulatory/claims risk.
6. Founder approves.
7. Track meeting, MoU, pilot, sponsorship, or joint program.

Deliverables:

- Partner map.
- Partnership thesis.
- Concept note.
- MoU term sheet draft.
- Stakeholder briefing.

## W5: Visibility Workflow

Applies to LinkedIn, press, podcasts, speaking, awards, events, newsletters, and
ecosystem exposure.

Flow:

1. Capture visibility opportunity or story signal.
2. Match to founder/product narrative.
3. Draft content, pitch, or submission.
4. Review claims and evidence.
5. Founder approves.
6. Publish/send/submit.
7. Track engagement and relationship outcomes.

Deliverables:

- LinkedIn post draft.
- Press pitch.
- Founder bio.
- Event/speaker application.
- Newsletter item.
- Media kit.

## W6: Investor Relations Workflow

Flow:

1. Segment investor by thesis, geography, stage, ticket, relationship path.
2. Prepare investor-specific narrative.
3. Draft warm intro request or direct outreach.
4. Prepare meeting agenda and desired outcome.
5. Log questions and diligence requests.
6. Send approved follow-up.
7. Add investor to monthly update cadence.

Deliverables:

- Investor profile.
- Intro request.
- Meeting prep.
- Diligence checklist.
- Investor update.

## W7: Collateral Factory Workflow

Flow:

1. Intake request: audience, objective, desired action, deadline.
2. Select template.
3. Pull product proof, metrics, screenshots, financials, and claims.
4. Draft asset.
5. Claims review.
6. Finance review if financial content appears.
7. Founder approval.
8. Save final link and version.

Deliverables:

- Pitch deck.
- One-pager.
- Concept note.
- Proposal.
- Case study.
- Data room index.
- Press/media kit.

## W8: Founder Command Center

Daily:

- New high-value signals.
- Replies needing action.
- Meetings today/tomorrow.
- Deadlines within 14 days.
- Drafts awaiting approval.
- Blockers.

Weekly:

- Pipeline health.
- New qualified opportunities.
- P1 opportunity movement.
- Outreach sent.
- Meetings booked.
- Proposals/applications submitted.
- Collateral produced.
- Visibility wins.
- Revenue/pilot progress.

## Status Model

| Stage | Meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| Signal | Captured but not qualified |
| Research | Needs analysis or eligibility check |
| Qualified | Worth pursuing |
| Drafting | Outreach, collateral, proposal, or application in progress |
| Approval | Awaiting founder/reviewer approval |
| Sent / Submitted | External action completed |
| Meeting | Meeting scheduled or completed |
| Diligence / Negotiation | Serious follow-up underway |
| Won | Funding, partnership, pilot, revenue, or visibility secured |
| Lost / Parked | Not pursuing now |
