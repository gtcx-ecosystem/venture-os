design the product around real portfolio needs rather than an abstract founder
persona.

## Common Pattern Across The Portfolio

The repos are different products, but they all need the same venture operating
motions:

1. Turn product capability into a buyer-specific narrative.
2. Identify capital, pilot, partner, and revenue opportunities.
3. Create tailored collateral quickly.
4. Track relationship paths and next actions.
5. Produce proof, diligence, and claims evidence.
6. Convert visibility into warm relationships.
7. Move from concept to paid pilot to scale agreement.

## Product Implication

Venture OS should not start as a fundraising-only tool. The portfolio needs a
broader venture growth system:

| Product Layer | Why It Matters |
| ------------- | -------------- |
| Capital Intelligence | Finds grants, VC, catalytic capital, DFIs, challenges, accelerators |
| Revenue Pipeline | Tracks buyers, pilots, enterprise deals, channel partners |
| Visibility Engine | Turns market intelligence into press, LinkedIn, newsletters, events |
| Investor Relations | Maintains CRM, updates, warm intros, diligence responses |
| Partnership Development | Handles DFIs, sovereigns, banks, telcos, corporates, universities |
| Collateral Factory | Decks, one-pagers, concept notes, proposals, data-room assets |
| Proof Layer | Claims, audit trails, evidence, compliance, metrics |
| Founder Command Center | Daily/weekly priorities, blockers, approvals, calendar, inbox |

## Design Constraint

The product must support African realities:

- Intermittent connectivity and mobile-first communication.
- Relationship-driven fundraising and partnership development.
- Cross-border capital sources and program eligibility.
- DFI, donor, foundation, bank, buyer, and VC pathways.
- WhatsApp, LinkedIn, Gmail, calendar, documents, and ClickUp-style execution.
- Need for credibility, proof, and world-class collateral despite small teams.
